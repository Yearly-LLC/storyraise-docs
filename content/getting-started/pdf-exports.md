# PDF exports

Web reports are great for sharing online, but sometimes you need a file — something to print, mail, attach to an email, or hand to your board. The **PDF Builder** is Storyraise's dedicated tool for designing and exporting polished, print-ready PDF documents.

## Opening the PDF Builder

From the dashboard, find the **Advanced PDF Builder** card under tools and click **Continue**. The PDF Builder opens in a new tab, where you'll see your recent documents and can create a new one.

## Setting up a document

When you create a document, you'll pick a page size and set up brand basics. You can change any of these later from the document title in the menu bar:

- **Format** — US Letter, US Legal, Tabloid, A3, A4, A5, Square, 5×7, 4×6 postcard, or custom dimensions in inches or millimeters.
- **Orientation** — portrait or landscape.
- **Brand fonts** — upload your own font files (.woff, .woff2, .ttf, or .otf) to use as defaults for headings and body text.
- **Logo and colors** — set your logo and brand colors so they're at hand while you design.

## Designing pages

Build each page by dragging items from the **Insert** panel onto the canvas — text, images, and other widgets — then fine-tune them in the **Styles** tab. Right-click any widget to duplicate it, lock it in place, reorder it (bring to front / send to back), or delete it. Pages can be organized into chapters as your document grows.

## Print-shop options

If you're sending your PDF to a professional printer, the PDF Builder includes the production settings they'll ask for:

- **Safe-area margin** — keeps important content away from the page edge.
- **Bleed** — extends backgrounds past the trim edge (none, ⅛", or ¼").
- **Crop marks** — printed guides showing where pages will be trimmed (available when bleed is on).
- **Page numbers** — toggle **Show page numbers in PDF** and pick a format (`1` or `1 / 12`).

For a PDF you'll only share digitally, you can leave bleed and crop marks off.

## Exporting

When your document is ready, open the menu bar and click **Export PDF**. Your browser downloads the finished PDF — fonts, images, and page numbers included.

## Tips

- **Design for the destination.** Pick your page size before you design — switching sizes later can shift your layouts.
- **Use real fonts.** Uploading your brand's actual font files ensures the exported PDF matches your printed materials exactly.
- **Proof before printing.** Print one copy from your own printer to check margins and colors before sending a large run to a print shop.
