# Navigation options

Published reports come with built-in navigation so readers can jump straight to what interests them — or simply scroll from top to bottom. This page covers what you can control.

## How report navigation works

Your report's **table of contents** — the ordered list of its sections — drives a navigation menu in the published report:

- On desktop, readers see a menu of your sections.
- On mobile, the menu collapses into a tap-to-open dropdown.
- The current section is highlighted as readers move through the report.

You don't build this menu by hand; it follows your sections automatically. Renaming or [reordering sections](reordering-sections.md) updates it.

## Section titles are menu labels

Each section's title is its label in the navigation menu. Keep titles short and scannable — "Our Impact" navigates better than "A Look Back at Everything We Accomplished Together This Year."

## Hiding sections from the menu

Sometimes a section belongs in the flow but not in the menu — a divider, a second gallery, a continuation of the previous topic. You can hide any section from the navigation menu while keeping it in the report; readers still reach it by scrolling.

## Navigation appearance

You can adjust the navigation's color scheme so the menu complements your report's design rather than clashing with a dark or vivid cover.

## Tips

- **Aim for 5–9 menu items.** If your report has fifteen sections, hide the supporting ones so the menu shows just the landmarks.
- **Check the mobile menu.** Long section titles truncate sooner on phones — preview in mobile view. See [Mobile optimization](mobile-optimization.md).
