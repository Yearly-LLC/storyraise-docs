Understanding report metrics

Once your report is published and shared, Insights shows you how it's performing. From the dashboard, open a published report's menu and choose View Insights. This page walks through every metric you'll see.

Two views: The Numbers and The Story

Insights has two tabs:

  • The Numbers — every metric, chart, and list, for when you want the detail.
  • The Story — an AI-written narrative summary of your report's performance, ready to read aloud in a staff meeting or export as a PDF for your board.

The headline metrics

  • Opens — total times your report has been viewed. Return visits count, so a report people come back to earns its number.
  • Constituents Reached — how many known constituents opened the report. This counts people identified through their personalized links; anonymous visits don't appear here.
  • Avg. Time Spent — how long visitors stay per visit. A strong signal of whether the report holds attention.
  • Avg. Sections Viewed — how far into the report visitors get on average.
  • Avg. Time per Section — how long readers spend on each section they reach.

Reading them together

The combinations matter more than any single number:

  • High Opens, low time spent — your distribution worked but the opening sections aren't holding people. Strengthen the first two sections.
  • High time spent, few sections viewed — readers are absorbed early but not progressing. Check whether a long early section needs splitting.
  • Opens far above Constituents Reached — most visits are anonymous; switch your mailings to personalized links to learn who's engaging.

Section Retention

The Section Retention diagram maps the path readers take between sections — and where visits end. Each square is a section (bigger = more readers), ribbons show the flow to the next section, and the red edge marks where visits stopped.

Use it to find your drop-off point: if half your readers leave after section three, that's where to tighten the report — or where to move the content you most need seen.

Engagement Heatmap and Visits Over Time

The Engagement Heatmap answers when did readers engage? — a grid of date × time of day (morning, afternoon, evening, night), with brighter cells meaning more activity. Range chips switch between the last 7, 14, 30, or more days, and the Visits Over Time trend chart follows the same range.

Use these to time your sends: if your audience reads in the evening, schedule next month's email for early evening, not 9 a.m.

Most Engaged Constituents

This list ranks the known constituents who engaged most deeply, each with:

  • An Engagement Score shown as stars:
    • ★ Attention — spent meaningfully more reading time than typical visitors
    • ★★ Depth — above-median time and viewed at least half the report
    • ★★★ Intent — all of the above plus a follow-up action, like clicking a call-to-action or returning for another visit
  • An intent signal showing their highest-value action — for example Clicked Donation CTA, Clicked Event RSVP, Clicked Volunteer, Watched YouTube, or Revisited Report.

This is your follow-up list: a ★★★ constituent who clicked your donation button is telling you something — a personal call or note within the week lands very differently than a quarterly blast.

Top Regions

A ranked list of where your views come from, by region. Useful for spotting unexpected reach — and for chapters or regional programs deciding where engagement is concentrated.

Ask the AI assistant

Below the metrics, the AI insights assistant answers questions about your report's performance in plain language. Try:

  • "Which cities had the highest engagement?"
  • "Which constituents viewed the most?"
  • "What section got the most views?"
  • "Give me a detailed summary of the report's performance."

A note on what the numbers don't include

Your own team's views are automatically excluded, so these metrics reflect your real audience — see Tracking engagement.
