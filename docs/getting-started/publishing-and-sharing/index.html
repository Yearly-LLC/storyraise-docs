Publishing and sharing reports

Until you publish, your report is a private draft only your team can see. Publishing puts it on the web at its own link, ready to share with donors, supporters, and stakeholders.

Publish your report

  1. Open your report in the builder.
  2. Click Publish in the top bar.
  3. The Ready to Publish? dialog shows the link where your report will live.
  4. Click Publish report!

Your report is now live. On the dashboard, its status changes from Draft to Published.

Your report's link

Every published report gets its own address based on your organization and the report title:

https://your-organization.yearly.report/your-report-title

This is the link you'll share everywhere. It works on desktop, tablet, and mobile — readers don't need an account or an app.

Updating a published report

Publishing isn't one-and-done. You can keep editing after you publish — your changes stay in draft until you push them live:

  1. Make your edits in the builder.
  2. Click Republish in the top bar.
  3. Confirm to publish your latest draft.

Readers always see the most recently published version; works-in-progress stay private until you republish.

Ways to share

  • Direct link. Find it anytime from the dashboard — open the report's menu and choose View Link — or copy it from the publish dialog.
  • Email and social. Paste the link into your email campaigns, newsletters, and social posts.
  • Embed on your website. Use an embed code to display the report inside a page on your own site.
  • QR codes. Generate QR codes that open your report — useful for printed mailers, event signage, and thank-you cards.
  • Personalized links. If you've imported constituent data, each constituent can get their own link (and QR code) that opens the report addressed to them.

Tracking engagement

Once a report is published, its dashboard card shows how many times it's been opened. For deeper engagement detail, open View Insights from the report's menu — see Tracking engagement and Understanding report metrics.
